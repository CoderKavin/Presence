App design language

A system for moments, not feeds.

Most social apps optimize for noise. Presence is built around its absence. The interface follows: warm surfaces, generous radii, editorial typography for memory, live signal for proximity. Quiet most of the time. Singular when something matters.

Foundation

The palette stays warm so the moments do.

Cream as the base, ink for type, saffron as the only accent. Surfaces nest from cream → recessed → elevated. Cinematic black appears once a year for the year-end film.

Form

Rounded, then more rounded.

Screens

Five screens, one philosophy.

Year-end film, the only reward
The cinematic mode. Black background appears once a year, signaling the artifact moment. Saffron is the only color allowed in. The year doesn't get scored or ranked. It just gets shown back.

Six rules for everything downstream.

  • Quiet by defaultNotifications only when a real person is at your door, your name is in a moment, or your year-end film is ready. Three reasons, total.
  • Identity without profileFriend color carries identity. No bio, no public profile, no follower count. You're known by the people who know you in person.
  • Italic is for memoryTimestamps, captions, anything looking back. Reserved, never decorative. Inter handles everything functional.
  • Saffron is for one thing onlyWhatever needs the user's eye next. No saffron borders for decoration. No saffron when nothing is happening.
  • Round the corners, all of them10 to 48 px depending on size. Sharp corners signal transactional, and Presence is the opposite of transactional.
  • One screen, one jobCapture is for capturing. Moments are for moments. Add friend is for adding friends. No screen does double duty, no UI fights for attention.
